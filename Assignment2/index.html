Serious Crimes In New York City

The Visualisation offers insights into the crime zones of Serious Criminal Offences and the trend of such crimes between January 2006 to December 2018 in New York City. Focus will be placed on 5 Serious Criminal Offences namely:

Murder • Rape • Theft-fraud • Kidnapping • Arson






New York City (NYC) located at the southern tip of the U.S. state is the most populous and densely populated city in the United States. With an estimated 2019 population of 8,336,817 distributed over about 302.6 square miles (784 km2). The city is the center of the New York metropolitan area, the largest metropolitan area in the world by urban landmass. (Wikipedia, n.d.)

Across the City

New York City is composed of five boroughs including:

• Bronx
• Manhattan
• Brooklyn
• Queens
• Staten Island




According to the dot map between 2006 to 2018, the most densely populated arrest zones for the serious crimes are located in the Bronx and Manhattan. The arrest spots for Rape, Arson and Kidnapping offences are distributed quite evenly throughout the city. The arrest spots for Murder offences are more concentrated around Bronx and Manhattan. Lastly, for Theft-fraud offences the arrest spots are densely gathered around the centre of Manhattan.





Distribution of Criminal Offences

Overall Trend

No drastic changes but slight fluctuations can noticed from the Streamgraph. Overall, the changes in the number of offences are consistent and a decreasing trend in the number of offences can be observed.








Crime rates in New York City spiked in the 1980s and early 1990s as the crack epidemic surged, but they have been dropping since 1991, and, as of 2017, they are among the lowest of major cities in the United States. (Wikipedia, n.d.)

The Most Committed Crimes

The most committed serious crime in New York City between Year 2006 to 2018 is Murder followed by Rape and Theft-Fraud Offences.

March 2008 has the maximum number of offences in a single month followed by October 2008.





Percentage Fall

There is a net decrement in the number of offences by 30.6% between Year 2006 to Year 2018 from 3251 to 2256 counts of serious criminal cases per year.


Maximum Fall

Between year 2008 and 2009 there is a maximum fall in the number of offences by nearly 12.7% (430 cases).





Biological Sex, Age Groups and Race

Crime by Gender

Between Year 2006 and 2018, out of the 5 serious crimes, the most commited crime for female is Theft-fraud and for male is Rape.

Male and Female

All categories of reported serious crime are commited more frequently by male than female in all all age-groups

Most Common Group

Most criminals of serious crime fall in the age-group of 25-44 despite their biological sex.

The Youth

Despite the smaller range for the 18-24 age-group, there is an immense amount (11448 cases) of serious crime committed by the group.





Racial Distribution



Approximately, half (50.6%) of the reported crime are commited by Black American followed by White Hispanic (25.3%) and White American (9.5%).
